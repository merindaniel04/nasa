🧠 Learn About Space

Anatomy of an Asteroid

Learn what terms like "Absolute Magnitude" and "Miss Distance" mean when tracking near-Earth objects.

  • Absolute Magnitude (H): A measure of an asteroid's brightness. The smaller the number, the brighter (and usually larger) the asteroid.
  • Miss Distance: The closest distance the asteroid will pass by Earth during its fly-by.
  • Potentially Hazardous: A designation for asteroids that are large enough and come close enough to Earth to be a potential impact threat in the future.

ISS 101

Key facts about the International Space Station, humanity's home in orbit.

  • Orbit Altitude: The ISS orbits at an average altitude of 400 kilometers (250 miles).
  • Speed: It travels at about 28,000 km/h (17,500 mph), completing one orbit of Earth in roughly 90 minutes.
  • Size: The station is as large as a football field and weighs almost 450,000 kilograms (1 million pounds).
